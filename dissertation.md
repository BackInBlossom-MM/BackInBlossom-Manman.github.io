# Quantifying the Impact of Weather-Related Risk on Agricultural and Financial Systems
**A Systematic and Explainable Machine Learning Approach**

*University of Edinburgh Business School | MSc in Data and Decision Analytics (2024/25)* *Word count: 16,225* ---

## Brief Summary

[cite_start]A primary manifestation of long-term climate change is the increased intensity, frequency, and duration of extreme weather events, which already poses a significant negative impact on global agriculture and leads to a long-term threat to worldwide food security[cite: 289, 290]. [cite_start]Furthermore, these extreme weather events do not only impact the physical world; they also affect economic and financial markets through a complex transmission pathway[cite: 291, 292]. 

[cite_start]To address the disconnect between physical climate studies and financial market analyses, this research breaks through the integration gap by building a systematic quantitative framework[cite: 295, 300]. [cite_start]By adopting an advanced XGBoost model combined with SHAP values, this study opens the machine learning "black-box" to quantify global and local feature contributions, investigating the complex non-linear interactions of compound weather events, and bridging the yield response signal in the physical world with the pricing mechanism of the financial market[cite: 299, 300, 301].
